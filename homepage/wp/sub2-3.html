Entry-level EMS

IoT-based UEBA concept entry-level EMS

It is the IoT-based UEBA concept entry-level EMS that has upgraded the existing AMI business area into a new UEBA concept EMS.

AMI technology

  • Data collection in the unit of 15 minutes
  • Remote metering of the amount used
  • Management of each unit device
  • Simple monitoring
  • Existing measurement business area
  • Short-term data management

Big data-based user behavior analysis technology

  • Real-time log collection
  • Behavioral pattern analysis
  • Various log correlation/linkage analyses
  • Abnormal behavior monitoring
  • New concept of security area
  • Long-term data management & analysis

IoT-based UEBA concept entry-level EMS

  • It is possible to have more effective and detailed energy use and management through the simple analysis of energy consumption and the detailed analysis of behavior or movement of user or device.
  • Application of UEBA concept of security area to AMI
  • Detailed electric power management adding the user behavioral pattern
  • Real-time measurement of various measurement data
  • Possible to discover various service models
  • Detection of device abnormal signs & linkage/correlation analysis
  • Various predictions through long-term data analysis

Expected effects

Energy cost reduction

Actual reduction of energy cost through energy use monitoring

Preliminary monitoring of device abnormal signs

Detection of device abnormal signs & support of preliminary preventive activities through the real-time electric power usage monitoring

Preliminary monitoring of electrical accidents

Prevention of safety accidents through the monitoring of electricity abnormal signs such as short circuit and spark

Checking parents’ recent condition

Checking the recent condition through the energy consumption analysis and monitoring of parents in hometown

Support for vulnerable class
(the aged living alone)

Strengthening the support for the detection of abnormal signs through the energy consumption analysis of vulnerable class
